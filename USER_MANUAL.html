Section 1

Getting Started

Installation, first launch, and initial configuration

System Requirements

  • Windows: Windows 10 or later
  • macOS: macOS 10.15 (Catalina) or later
  • Storage: 200 MB free disk space
  • Internet: Not required (needed only for email and online payments)

Installation

  1. Download OwnInvoice Desktop from your purchase email or the OwnInvoice website.
  2. Run the installer. On macOS, drag the app to your Applications folder. On Windows, follow the setup wizard.
  3. Launch OwnInvoice Desktop. The application will create a local database on first run.
  4. Enter your license key when prompted to activate the full version.

Initial Setup

After launching OwnInvoice for the first time, head to Settings to configure your company information:

  1. Enter your company name, address, email, and phone number. This information appears on all invoices.
  2. Upload your company logo. It will be displayed on invoices and quotes.
  3. Set your tax rate and currency under Tax & Currency settings.
  4. Configure your invoice numbering format (prefix, starting number).
  5. Set up email (SMTP) if you want to send invoices and reminders by email.
Tip

Your data is stored locally on your computer. No cloud account or internet connection is required for core invoicing features.

Section 3

Invoices

Create, manage, and track professional invoices

Creating an Invoice

  1. Click New Invoice from the Invoices page or press Ctrl+N.
  2. Select a client from the dropdown or create a new one inline.
  3. The invoice number is auto-generated based on your numbering settings. You can edit it if needed.
  4. Set the invoice date and due date.
  5. Add line items with description, quantity, rate, and optional discount per item.
  6. Optionally add shipping costs, adjustments, and notes.
  7. Click Save to save as a draft, or Save & Send to email it to the client.

Line Items

Each line item on an invoice supports:

  • Description — What you're billing for
  • Quantity — Number of units
  • Rate — Price per unit
  • Discount — Per-item discount (percentage or fixed amount)
  • SKU — Stock keeping unit for inventory tracking
  • Unit of Measure — Each, Hour, Day, Meter, Kilogram, etc.

You can link items from your Saved Items library for quick entry.

Invoice Totals

  • Subtotal — Sum of all line items
  • Discount — Invoice-level discount (percentage or fixed)
  • Tax — Calculated from your tax rate setting
  • Shipping — Optional shipping/delivery cost
  • Adjustment — Positive or negative amount with a custom label
  • Total — Final amount due

Invoice Statuses

Draft

Work in progress. Not yet sent to the client.

Pending

Sent to the client, awaiting payment.

Paid

Payment received in full.

Overdue

Past the due date. Automatically detected.

Batch Operations

Select multiple invoices using the checkboxes to perform bulk actions:

  • Mark as Paid — Update selected invoices to paid status
  • Archive — Move selected invoices to the archive
  • Delete — Permanently remove selected invoices

Filtering & Sorting

Use the status filter tabs (All, Draft, Pending, Paid, Overdue) to view specific invoice groups. Use the search bar to find invoices by number or client name.

Section 4

Quotes & Estimates

Create proposals and convert them to invoices

Creating a Quote

Quotes work just like invoices, with a few differences:

  • Quotes have a separate numbering system (e.g., QT-0001)
  • Quotes include an expiry date instead of a due date
  • Quotes use statuses: Draft, Sent, Accepted, Rejected

To create a quote, navigate to the Quotes section and click New Quote. Fill in the client, items, and amounts as you would for an invoice.

Converting a Quote to an Invoice

When a client accepts your quote, click the Convert to Invoice button. OwnInvoice will create a new invoice with all the quote's details pre-filled. The original quote is marked as accepted.

Tip

The converted invoice links back to the original quote for easy reference. You can find this link in the invoice details.

Archiving Quotes

Old or rejected quotes can be archived to keep your active list clean. Archived quotes can be restored at any time.

Section 5

Credit Notes

Issue refunds and adjustments

What are Credit Notes?

Credit notes are used to issue partial or full refunds, correct billing errors, or provide credits to clients. Each credit note is linked to a specific invoice.

Creating a Credit Note

  1. Navigate to Credit Notes and click New Credit Note.
  2. Select the invoice the credit applies to.
  3. Choose a reason (refund, return, adjustment, billing error, etc.).
  4. Add the items being credited with quantities and amounts.
  5. Save as draft or issue immediately.

Credit Note Statuses

  • Draft — Not yet issued to the client
  • Issued — Applied to the invoice
Section 6

Client Management

Manage your customers and their information

Adding a Client

Navigate to Clients and click New Client. Enter the client's details:

  • Name (required) and Email (required)
  • Phone number
  • Address (street, city, state, zip)
  • Website, Industry, Company Size
  • Contact person and title
  • Secondary contact information
  • Tax ID and tax exempt status
  • Notes for internal reference

Customer Numbers

Each client can have a unique customer number (e.g., CUST-0001). Enable customer numbers in Settings and choose your prefix.

Credit Management

Set credit limits and payment terms per client:

  • Credit Limit — Maximum outstanding balance allowed
  • Payment Terms — NET 30, NET 60, Due on Receipt, etc.
  • Default Discount Rate — Automatic discount for this client

Multiple Addresses

Each client can have multiple billing and shipping addresses. Label them (Main, Warehouse, Branch Office, etc.) and mark one as default. When creating an invoice, select which address to use.

CSV Import

Import clients in bulk from a CSV file. OwnInvoice validates the data and reports any errors before importing.

Client Statistics

View per-client statistics including total invoices, revenue, outstanding balance, and last invoice date.

Section 7

Saved Items

Your reusable product and service catalog

What are Saved Items?

Saved Items are a library of products and services you frequently bill for. Instead of typing item details every time, select from your saved items when creating an invoice.

Creating a Saved Item

  • Description — Name/description of the product or service
  • Rate — Default selling price
  • Category — General, Consulting, Development, Design, Marketing, Support, or Other
  • SKU — Stock Keeping Unit for tracking
  • Barcode — Product barcode if applicable
  • Unit of Measure — Each, Hour, Day, Meter, Kilogram, Liter, etc.
  • Cost Price — Your cost (used for markup calculation)
  • Markup % — Desired markup percentage. The selling rate is auto-calculated from cost + markup.
  • Taxable — Whether tax applies to this item
  • Active — Active items appear in the item selector on invoices

Using Saved Items

When adding line items to an invoice or quote, click the saved items selector to browse your catalog. Selecting an item auto-fills the description, rate, SKU, and unit of measure.

CSV Import

Import your product catalog from a CSV file for quick setup.

Section 8

Recurring Invoices

Automate your regular billing

Setting Up a Recurring Invoice

  1. Navigate to Recurring Invoices and click New Recurring Invoice.
  2. Select a client and give the recurring template a name.
  3. Choose a frequency: Weekly, Bi-weekly, Monthly, Quarterly, or Yearly.
  4. Set the start date and optional end date.
  5. Add your line items with descriptions, quantities, and rates.
  6. Add notes and payment terms as needed.
  7. Click Save to activate.

How It Works

OwnInvoice automatically checks for due recurring invoices every hour and on app startup. When a recurring invoice's next generation date arrives, a new invoice is created with:

  • A new auto-generated invoice number
  • Today's date as the invoice date
  • Due date set to 30 days from today
  • All line items copied from the recurring template
  • Status set to Pending

After generation, the next generation date advances by the selected frequency.

Manual Generation

Click the Generate Now button on any active recurring invoice to immediately create an invoice from that template.

Managing Recurring Invoices

  • Edit — Update items, frequency, or dates
  • Deactivate — Pause generation without deleting
  • Delete — Remove the recurring template entirely
Section 9

Reminders

Automated and manual payment reminders

Reminder Templates

OwnInvoice includes three default reminder templates. You can edit these or create new ones:

  • Payment Due Soon — Sent 3 days before the due date
  • Payment Overdue — Sent 7 days after the due date
  • Second Reminder — Sent 14 days after the due date

Template Variables

Use these variables in your email subject and body. They are automatically replaced with actual values:

VariableReplaced With
{invoice_number}The invoice number (e.g., INV-1001)
{client_name}The client's name
{total}The invoice total amount
{due_date}The invoice due date
{company_name}Your company name

Sending Reminders Manually

  1. Go to the Reminders section and select the Send Reminders tab.
  2. You'll see a list of invoices that are due soon or overdue.
  3. Click a template button next to any invoice to send that reminder.
  4. Review the preview of the email that will be sent.
  5. Click Send Reminder. The email is sent via SMTP with the invoice PDF attached.

Automatic Reminders

OwnInvoice automatically checks for invoices needing reminders every 6 hours and on app startup. It matches invoices to the appropriate template based on the days before or after the due date, and sends the email automatically. Each template is only sent once per invoice to prevent spam.

Reminder History

View all sent reminders in the History tab, showing which template was used, when it was sent, and to which invoice.

Note

Email reminders require SMTP to be configured in Settings. See the Email section for setup instructions.

Section 11

Payments & Payment Gateways

Record payments and accept online payments

Recording a Payment

When you receive payment for an invoice:

  1. Open the invoice and click Record Payment.
  2. Enter the amount received.
  3. Select the payment method (Cash, Check, Bank Transfer, Credit Card, etc.).
  4. Enter a reference number (check number, transaction ID, etc.).
  5. Add any notes and click Save.

An invoice can have multiple partial payments. OwnInvoice automatically updates the invoice status to Paid when the total payments equal or exceed the invoice total.

Payment Gateways

OwnInvoice integrates with 5 payment gateways to let your clients pay online. Configure them in Settings > Payment Gateways:

Stripe

Accept credit/debit cards and more. Enter your API keys and enable.

PayPal

Accept PayPal payments. Enter Client ID and Secret.

Square

Accept Square payments. Enter Access Token and Location ID.

GoCardless

Accept direct debit payments. Enter Access Token.

Authorize.Net is also supported with API Login ID and Transaction Key.

Each gateway supports sandbox/test mode for testing before going live. Generate payment links from any invoice to share with your clients.

Section 12

Email

Send invoices and reminders by email

SMTP Configuration

To send emails from OwnInvoice, configure your SMTP settings in Settings > Email Configuration:

  • SMTP Host — Your email server (e.g., smtp.gmail.com)
  • SMTP Port — Usually 587 (TLS) or 465 (SSL)
  • Secure — Enable for SSL connections (port 465)
  • Username — Your email address or SMTP username
  • Password — Your email password or app-specific password
  • From Name — The name that appears in the "From" field
  • From Email — The email address that appears in the "From" field
Gmail Users

Use smtp.gmail.com with port 587. You'll need to generate an App Password in your Google Account settings (Security > 2-Step Verification > App Passwords).

Sending an Invoice by Email

  1. Open an invoice and click Send Email.
  2. The recipient is pre-filled with the client's email. Add CC/BCC if needed.
  3. Edit the subject and body as needed (template variables are supported).
  4. Click Send. The invoice PDF is automatically attached.

Email Templates

Configure default email templates in Settings. Use the same template variables as reminders ({invoice_number}, {client_name}, {total}, etc.).

PDF & Printing

Export professional invoices as PDF documents

Saving as PDF

Open any invoice and click Download PDF. OwnInvoice generates a professionally formatted PDF with your company branding, logo, and colors.

PDF Customization

Control the look of your PDFs in Settings:

  • Page Size — Letter, A4, Legal
  • Margins — Small, Normal, Large
  • Header Height — Compact, Normal, Spacious
  • Logo — Show or hide your logo on invoices
  • Company Address — Show or hide on invoices
  • Border Style — Subtle, Bold, or None

Invoice Template Styles

Choose from multiple invoice layouts and customize the appearance:

  • Template — Modern, Classic, and more
  • Header Style — Left-aligned, centered, or right-aligned
  • Table Style — Striped, gridded, or plain
  • Fonts — Separate heading and body font selection
  • Colors — Invoice accent color, header color, text colors
  • Corner Style — Rounded or square
  • Spacing — Compact, normal, or spacious

What Appears on the Invoice

Control which fields appear on printed invoices in Settings > Invoice Display:

  • Client email, phone, billing address, shipping address, tax ID
  • Item SKU and unit of measure
  • Customer numbers
  • Tax breakdown
  • Payment terms and bank details
Section 14

Settings & Configuration

Customize OwnInvoice to fit your business

Company Information

Set your company name, address, contact details, tax ID, and business registration number. Upload your logo for use on invoices.

Invoice Numbering

  • Prefix — Text before the number (e.g., INV-)
  • Suffix — Text after the number
  • Starting Number — Where numbering begins
  • Quote Prefix — Separate prefix for quotes (e.g., QT-)

Tax & Currency

  • Tax Rate — Default tax percentage applied to invoices
  • Tax Label — "Tax", "VAT", "GST", etc.
  • Currency Symbol and Currency Code
  • Number Formatting — Decimal and thousand separators
  • Date Format — MM/DD/YYYY, DD/MM/YYYY, YYYY-MM-DD, etc.

Payment Defaults

  • Default Due Days — Number of days until payment is due (default: 30)
  • Payment Terms — Default terms text on invoices
  • Bank Details — Bank account info displayed on invoices
  • Invoice Footer — Custom footer message (e.g., "Thank you for your business!")

Theme & Appearance

  • Dark Mode — Off, On, or follow system preference
  • Color Palette — Primary, secondary, accent colors for the app
  • Invoice Colors — Header color, accent color, text colors for PDFs
  • Fonts — Heading font and body font selection

Navigation Customization

Choose which sidebar tabs are visible and reorder them to match your workflow. Enable or disable sections like Quotes, Credit Notes, Recurring Invoices, Reports, etc.

Database

OwnInvoice uses SQLite by default (no setup required). For advanced use, you can connect to MySQL, PostgreSQL, or SQL Server by entering your database connection details.

Section 15

Backup & Restore

Protect your business data

Automatic Backups

Configure automatic backups in Settings > Backup:

  • Enable/Disable automatic backups
  • Schedule — Daily, weekly, or monthly
  • Time — What time of day to run the backup
  • Day — Day of week (weekly) or day of month (monthly)
  • Retention — How many days to keep old backups
  • Location — Choose where backup files are saved

Manual Backup

Create a backup at any time by clicking Backup Now in the Backup settings.

Restoring from Backup

To restore from a backup, click Restore and select a backup file. This will replace your current database with the backed-up version.

Important

Restoring from a backup replaces all current data. Make sure to create a fresh backup before restoring if you want to preserve your current data.

Section 16

Archive

Manage old invoices and quotes

Archiving

Archive invoices and quotes you no longer need in your active lists. Archived items are moved to the Archive section and won't appear in your main views or dashboard stats.

Restoring

Archived items can be restored at any time. Navigate to the Archive section, find the item, and click Restore.

Permanent Deletion

From the Archive, you can permanently delete items. This action cannot be undone.

Archive Stats

The Archive section shows the total count and combined amount of all archived invoices.

Global Search

Invoice Filtering
